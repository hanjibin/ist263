Inner Landscapes: Cellular Worlds

This series explores how the invisible processes inside the body and my understandings of cellular biology can become a metaphor for personal experience. You can enjoy my biological imagery to communicate my emotional and physical journey.

Selected Works

Cell Project

Description: Cellkoyaki is a word I created by combining "cells" and "takoyaki." Takoyaki has always been my soul food. It was something my parents often ordered for me when I was home alone after school. I would sit there, waiting for them to return, eating a whole box by myself. At the same time, my fascination with biology led me to repeatedly draw and craft the shapes of eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells, until they felt like friendly, hand-made toys. Merging these two nostalgic parts of my life, I came up with "Cellkoyaki"---a playful blend of memory, food, and science.



Description: I transformed a shoe into a mouth part of discomfort, turning the tongue into a realistic clay tongue marked with canker sores. On the outer surface, I added painful pimples to evoke a sense of disturbance and unease. For me, stomatitis often appears when I'm tired, and the pain is both sharp and irritating. I wanted to symbolize my exhaustion and fatigue by exposing both the inner and outer mouth. I was also struck by the idea that a "shoe tongue" shares its name with the human tongue. Through this piece, I wanted to share that sensation, inviting the audience to feel empathy for these small but intense moments of the body's vulnerability.



Description: I sketched myself in five poses, outlined in white against the black paper, as if tracing moments of my own presence. My limbs are colored in shades of blue and green, while my face and body are warmed with yellows, reds, and purples. The contrast mirrors what I live with: even in summer, my hands and feet stay cold, and when winter comes, it is unbearable.



Description: This piece reflects my focus on the sound of my own heartbeat, where even the smallest change made me anxious. During one of the most difficult times in my life---when I was diagnosed with hypokalemia and arrhythmia---I created a headphone that allowed me to listen to my heart and kidney sounds, as if I could detect any error within my body. This vividly capture the most nervous and challenging moment in my life.



Description: I observed that the trunk of an elephant and the tentacle of an octopus shows similar movement and function when grabbing something. Then, I found it interesting to replace elephant's trunk with an octopus leg.



Description: In this work, I transform the microscopic world of soil into a tangible experience. Inspired by an egg-shaped lampshade from the Bird's Special Collection and the hidden life of prokaryotes, I created an oval structure representing a single cell. Using coffee filters to capture and filter soil samples, I painted delicate watercolors on them to reveal the unseen beauty of these tiny organisms. The black face mask on the "flagellum" hints at their impact on humans, blending science and art in a tangible, thought-provoking form.
